Predators

Natural predators of the moose include bears, tigers and wolves. These animals target calves with the adult males using their large antlers to defend themselves.



​ Humans impact their population through forestry and agriculture which decreases the availability of their preferred forests.



Parasites such as ticks can also become a major threat. They will attach in large numbers to a moose potentially causing anemia. In an attempt to rid themselves of the ticks they will rub off patches of fur.



Some hunting occurs. In areas this may be to reduce their impact on forestry while in others it is for sport. They are also commonly impacted by vehicle strikes both on roads and trainlines.


The Wild Javi

The moose's biggest predator today is the wild Javi. This monster of an animal can consume up to 10 million times its own weight. Therefore it is a large threat to every other animal.



On one occasion a wild Javi was seen to have eaten a total of 67 mooses and 78 tonnes of wood. The USAF had to use rockets to safely remove the threat to neighbouring towns and villages.



Despite being so powerful, the wild Javi is an extremely endangered species and has not been sighted for the past 12 years. This is mainly due to the fact that these creatures are so greedy that by the age of only 10 years, they will attempt to eat themselves. If they manage to do so (which is usually the case) the only thing remaining would be its tummy – this contains the rest of the chewed up body.
